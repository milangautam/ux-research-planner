# Alternative Research Frameworks

If the researcher uses a framework other than OMSEP, adapt module names and structure while preserving the underlying logic. Here are common alternatives:

## Google's HEART Framework
**For:** Measuring user experience quality across five dimensions
- **Happiness** — user satisfaction (surveys, NPS)
- **Engagement** — level of user involvement (behavioral metrics)
- **Adoption** — new users starting to use a feature
- **Retention** — users returning over time
- **Task Success** — efficiency and error rate

Map to OMSEP: Use HEART dimensions to define research goals (O), then proceed with the same M, S, E, P modules.

## Double Diamond
**For:** Design process framing (Discover → Define → Develop → Deliver)
Research planning typically lives in the **Discover** and **Define** phases.
Map to OMSEP: Objectives and Methodology align to Discover; Scope and Essentials align to Define.

## Jobs To Be Done (JTBD) Research Planning
**For:** Understanding what users are "hiring" a product to do
Research goals become: "What job is the user trying to get done?" and "What are the functional, social, and emotional dimensions of that job?"
Map to OMSEP: Frame research goals (O) using JTBD language; methods and the rest of the modules remain the same.

## Lean UX Research
**For:** Fast-cycle research in agile environments
Prioritize: 1 research goal per sprint, 3–5 participants, rapid synthesis (same day)
Reduce Essentials (E) to minimum viable scope — fewer deliverables, faster timeline.

## How to Adapt
When the researcher specifies a non-OMSEP framework:
1. Acknowledge the framework they use
2. Map their framework's phases to OMSEP's underlying logic (objectives → methods → scope → essentials → protocol)
3. Use their terminology in the output (e.g., call "Objectives" "Goals" or "Hypotheses" if that's their language)
4. Keep all quality standards (bias-free questions, screener best practices, etc.) unchanged — these apply regardless of framework
